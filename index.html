About BBS coin

BBS coin is a cryptocurrency with it's own blockchain for transactions, that allows webmasters to offer BBS coins as a point exchange/swap perk on their websites or forums for their members. Members who opt-in can then exchange their BBS coins with other members or withdraw to their own BBS wallet for holding(also known as hodl) or may send it to their account's BBS address on one of the Exchanges where BBS coin is listed on to exchange for other cryptocurrencies like BitCoin(BTC) and LiteCoin(LTC). We use Cryptonight Lite v7 as the algorithm for the software.

Because BBS is a coin then it makes it mineable, meaning you could share your computing power with a BBS mining pool(see under ECOSYSTEM) to help find new blocks on the blockchain which holds the records of newly created transactions and help confirm them by verifying the balance of the senders of the transactions with previous blocks. You will get a share of the reward for helping find the next block (Check the current 'Reward' under the next section).

Basic Node Statistics

On this list we will try to explain the basic live statistics you will see around the BBS Coin network of websites like on the main page, the mining pool, and the blockchain explorer.

A BBS Node is a software designed to hold a copy of the BBS blockchain and facilitate transactions.
Hash Rate is how much calculating power all miners are using to solve the next block.
Height is the number of the last found block. Each block has a batch containing transactions.
Diffulty is calculated depending on the Hash Rate to make it harder or easier to find a new block.
Reward is the amount of BBS that will be awarded to the mining pool that finds the new block.
Emission is the Supply in percentage from the total possible supply of 184,470,000,000.0 BBS.
Supply is the amount of BBS calculated to be currently available from mining.
Transactions is the amount of transactions that are listed on the blockchain.

On-Blockchain Transactions

This graphic displays the total amount of BBS transfarred per block in a 24 hour lifespan, these are known to be as on-chain or on-blockchain transactions. We have excluded the block reward transactions from this graphic, that is why there are some blocks missing as such blocks had no additional transactions attached to them besides the block reward. Also transactions inside the exchanges are not recorded on the blockchains as they happen within their system or BBS wallet and are known as off-chain transactions. On-chain transactions do include deposit/withdrawal transfers to/from exchanges and other transfers or movement of BBS coins.
